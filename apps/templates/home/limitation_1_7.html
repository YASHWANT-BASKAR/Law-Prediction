      SUITS RELATING TO TORT

S.No. Description of suits Period of limitation Time from which period begins to run
1. For compensation for doing or for omitting to do an act alleged to be in pursuance of any enact­ment in force for the time being in the territories to which this Act extends. One years When the act or omis­sion takes place.
2. For compensation for false imprisonment. One years When the imprison­ment ends.
3. For compensation for a malicious prosecution One years When the plaintiff is acquitted or the prose­cution is otherwise ter­minated.
4. For compensation for libel One years When the libel is pub­lished.
5. For compensation for slander One years When the words are spoken or, if the words are not actionable in themselves, when the special damage com­plained of results.
6. For compensation for loss of service occasioned by the seduction of the plaintiff’s servant or daughter. One years When the loss occurs.
7. For compensation for inducing a person to break a contract with the plaintiff. One years The date of the breach.
8. For compensation for wrongful seizure of movable property under legal process. One years The date of the seizure
9. By executors, administrators or representatives under the Legal Representatives’ Suits Act, 1855 (12 of 1855). One years The date of the death of the person wronged.
10. By executors, administrators or representatives under the Indian Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 (13 of 1855). Two years The date of the death of the person killed.
11. Under the Legal Representatives’ Suits Act, 1855 (12 of 1855) against an executor, an administrator or any other representative. Two years When the wrong com­plained of is done
12. Against one who, having a right to use property for specific pur­poses, perverts it to other pur­poses. Two years When the perversion first becomes known to the person injured thereby.
13. For compensation for obstructing a way or a water course. Three years The date of the obstruc­tion.
14. For compensation for diverting a water course. Three years The date of the diversion.
15. By executors, administrators or representatives under the Indian Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 (13 of 1855). Three years The date of the trespass
16. By executors, administrators or representatives under the Indian Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 (13 of 1855). Three years The date of the infringement.
17. Against one who, having a right to use property for specific pur­poses, perverts it to other pur­poses. Three years When the waste begins.
18. For compensation for injury caused by an injunction wrong­fully obtained. Three years When the injunction ceases.
19. For compensation, for wrongfully taking or detaining any specific movable property lost, or acquired by theft, or dis­honest mis-appropriation or conversion Three years When the person hav­ing the right to the possession of the pro­perty first learns in whose possession it is.
20. For compensation, for wrongfully taking or inj uring or wrongfully detain­ing any other specific mov­able property. Three years When the property is wrongfully taken or injured, or when the detainer’s possession becomes unlawful
